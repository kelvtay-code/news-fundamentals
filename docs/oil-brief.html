Overall Market Summary
Jul 15 was a partial unwind of the Jul 14 Hormuz-driven spike, layered under an even sharper rhetorical escalation. Iran's threat to block all regional oil and gas exports is the most extreme statement of the crisis so far, but the physical tape told a different story: Hormuz traffic nearly doubled (18→34 ships), and two major bypass routes — UAE's Habshan pipeline to Fujairah and Saudi's Yanbu Red Sea terminal — both posted record or near-record June export volumes. Crude itself cooled (cash Dubai -$3.91/b, premium to futures roughly halved) as Asian refiners piled into ~17mn bbl of discounted WTI Midland rather than chase Gulf barrels. Products split along the same fault line as Jul 14: Gasoil and HSFO/Marine Fuel structure continued tightening (gasoil backwardation stayed near multi-week highs on fresh Russian refinery drone strikes; LSFO cash premiums hit 3-week highs on a genuine Singapore cargo shortage), while Jet Kero's paper curve eased as the US reversed its Hormuz transit-fee plan. Naphtha is the standout: FGE's independent monthly fundamentals report confirms the Platts MOC price action (~$100-115/t MOPJ-Brent cracks, up from a $70/t June average) is being driven as much by Western tightness — US gasoline blending pull, critically low Rhine water levels choking German barge logistics, and new Russian rules that could trap naphtha-octane blending domestically — as by Middle East risk premium. Two large gasoline/naphtha tenders surfaced this session (Iraq SOMO's 668,250mt gasoline tender and confirmation of AMEC/Hanwha TotalEnergies naphtha tenders), the first meaningful tender-level demand confirmation in two sessions.

📅 FGE Naphtha Monthly — Structural Context (July 2026 edition)

FGE's independent monthly report, compiled around the same window, corroborates the Platts price action with several structural threads not visible in the daily MOC tape alone:

  • • MOPJ naphtha/Brent cracks climbed past $100/t at the start of July after averaging just $70/t in June — renewed US-Iran tensions "abated concerns over a deluge of Middle East outflows" that had pressured the complex the prior month. East-West spread widened above $40/t; M1/M2 timespread moved "well above the teens."
  • • Middle East net naphtha exports slipped to ~900kt in June (m-o-m) on higher inflows to Yanbu, Fujairah, Hamriyah, and Sohar — this is the supply-side mirror of the demand-side UAE/Saudi bypass-route story in today's Oilgram crude piece. FGE flags Kuwait's Mina Al-Ahmadi, Saudi's SATORP (240kb/d CDU), and Bahrain's Sitra (170kb/d CDU) as structurally capped until 2027.
  • • Russia's naphtha exports face a new domestic constraint: persistent drone strikes have catalyzed rules treating naphtha-octane blending as a "refundable refining process," which may keep volumes captive inside Russia rather than exported — a genuine, non-obvious bullish supply thread that reinforces gasoil's Russia-driven bullish case elsewhere in this brief.
  • • Rhine River levels (also flagged independently in today's Oilgram) are constraining ARA/German naphtha and reformate movement, keeping European high-octane premiums elevated and, per FGE, pulling more naphtha into the domestic gasoline blending pool rather than export.
  • • Chinese buying interest is real but price-sensitive: Unipec tested OSN cargoes for August/September but cancelled both due to high premiums — consistent with today's zero Middle East/China cargo trades but a live bid book.
🌍 Geopolitical Factor

Iran struck US military targets at Bahrain's Sheikh Isa Air Base and Kuwait's Ali Al-Salem Air Base/Mina Abdullah — explicitly cited by Platts as the reason the naphtha market was "expected to strengthen July 15." The spread between Russian and non-Russian naphtha is heard narrowing following strikes on Russian refineries in H1 July.

Rationale: Naphtha now has the strongest multi-source corroboration of any product in this brief — Platts' MOC (first confirmed cargo trade, two live tenders) and FGE's independent monthly fundamentals (crack widening from $70/t to $100+/t, structurally capped Middle East supply, a new Russian export constraint) all point the same direction. Upgrade conviction from Jul 14's "thin MOC confirmation" — the FGE report closes the gap between price and physical evidence that was the main caveat yesterday.
🟡 JET KERO — NEUTRAL/EASING (REVERSAL FROM JUL 14)
⚠️ Paper curve eased as US reversed the Hormuz transit-fee plan
📊 MOC Activity

Price fell sharply, -$9.89/b to $148.15/b, unwinding most of Jul 14's +$28.51/b spike. The Aug-Sep swap time spread — which hit an 8-week high of $8.58/b at the Jul 14 close — eased to $6.98/b in Jul 15 midafternoon trading. One confirmed paper trade (Vitolsg→Dare, 50kb, $141.90/bbl); physical book carries just one offer (BP, MOPS+$10.00, 100kb) and zero bids or trades.

🌍 Geopolitical & Demand Factors

Sentiment eased "slightly" in the paper segment specifically because the US reversed plans to charge fees for securing the Strait of Hormuz. A North Asia-based trader noted "August [supply] is okay, but September and October are in question." Middle East spot premiums for FOB Arab Gulf cargoes were heard firmer on escalating hostilities, but delivered cargoes inside the Strait are reportedly under pressure as traffic stays limited. Vietnam's jet imports hit a 6-month high in June (201,629mt, +26.41% y/y).

Rationale: Jet Kero shows the clearest day-over-day reversal in this brief — both price and swap-spread structure retreated from Jul 14 highs on a specific, identifiable catalyst (the US fee reversal), not a change in the underlying supply risk. Downgrade from Jul 14's "Bullish, high conviction" to Neutral — the easing appears to be a genuine sentiment shift, not just profit-taking, but the underlying Hormuz risk (Iran's export-blockade threat) has not gone away.

🏭 Supply Disruption — the key new driver

Ukrainian drone attacks hit Russia's Salavat refinery in the early hours of Jul 14, causing a fire. All three refineries in the Samara hub — Syzran, Kuybishev, Novokuybishev — are now offline as a result of the latest and previous strikes. This directly extends Jul 14's Russia export-ban narrative into a fresh, unplanned supply-side shock landing on the same product two days running.

📋 Tender Activity

Vietnam's Petrolimex sought 40,000mt of 500ppm sulfur gasoil for delivery Jul 21-30 — first confirmed gasoil buy tender in the window.

Rationale: Two independent supply shocks (Russia's export ban + fresh Salavat/Samara refinery strikes) are now compounding on top of already-tight Market Structure and the heaviest MOC volume of any product. Hold Bullish/High — this is the most persistent, multi-session call in the brief, now with a specific fresh catalyst (Salavat) rather than just carrying forward yesterday's read.

📦 Physical Confirmation — Singapore Bunker Data

Singapore bunker sales hit a 3-month high in June (4.669mn mt, +2.7% m/m, +1.6% y/y). HSFO sales climbed to a 5-month high (2.04mn mt, +17.1% y/y, +12.9% m/m), now 43.7% of total volume. LSFO sales, by contrast, fell to their lowest since Feb 2025 (2.227mn mt) — a record-low 47.7% share of total bunker volume since the IMO 2020 sulfur cap took effect. This is a genuine structural grade shift, not noise: a severe LSFO cargo shortage pushed the Singapore-delivered 0.5%S bunker premium to an average of $67.78/mt in June (more than tripling from May's $20.88/mt), and further to $82.51/mt over Jul 1-14.

Rationale: HSFO/LSFO carries the strongest real-economy confirmation in this brief — the cash-to-premium flip, the confirmed physical trades, and independently-sourced Singapore bunker statistics (a genuine multi-month structural LSFO shortage) all corroborate each other. Highest-confidence call alongside Gasoil — this is not sentiment-driven, it shows up in actual bunker delivery volumes.
