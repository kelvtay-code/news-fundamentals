Overall Market Summary
Jul 14 was a geopolitically-driven, supply-risk session across the complex: the Hormuz tanker attacks and blockade reinstatement drove crude and freight sharply higher (cash Dubai +$10.90/b, Dubai curve into backwardation, WTI/Brent at one-month highs), and that risk premium showed up asymmetrically across products depending on how directly each depends on Persian Gulf-loading logistics. Gasoil and HSFO/Marine Fuel structure tightened the most — gasoil's Aug-Sep swap backwardation surged 51% d/d to $8.30/b (near a 2-month high) and 380 CST HSFO paper carried the heaviest confirmed trade volume of the session (5 physical trades, dozens of paper prints). Gasoline was comparatively soft — the FOB Singapore 92 RON cash differential nearly halved w/w to 74¢/b on North Asian (China/Korea) cargo emergence, and the Singapore 92 RON physical MOC showed active bid withdrawals rather than fresh buying. Naphtha and Jet Kero physical cargo markets were quiet (bids only, no confirmed physical trades), consistent with Persian Gulf-loading MOC data remaining suppressed under Platts' standing Hormuz-transit restriction. The one confirmed tender in the window — Iraq's SOMO offering 432,000mt of naphtha for H2 2026 (closes Jul 23) — sits against a backdrop of a source caution that ship scarcity could limit naphtha movement through the Strait regardless of price.

🟢 GASOLINE — NEUTRAL/SOFTENING
⚠️ Supply emergence outweighing geo premium
📊 MOC Activity

FOB Singapore Cargo (physical): one confirmed trade — TRADE PTT sold 100kb (Aug 4-8) to Vitol at $107.40/b. Offers from SKEISG at $107.10/b (Aug 3-7) and $107.00/b (Aug 8-12). Notably heavy Vitol bid withdrawals across five+ laycans ($106.80-$108.00/b, both outright and MOPS 92 differential form) — a bearish tell that the buy side pulled back rather than chased price. PTT also withdrew its own offer.

FOB Singapore Paper (Mogas 92, Aug26): one confirmed trade, Dare sold 25kb to Onyx at $103.30/b, inside an active $102.40-$104.40/b bid/offer ladder (Onyx, Hotei, Neruda, Dare, Vitolsg, Freeptasia). Paper spreads (Bal Month/Aug, Aug/Sep, Sep/Oct) saw SKEISG sell the Bal Month/Aug spread 3x to Hotei at $4.05/b.

📋 Tender Activity

No FOB Singapore or Arab Gulf gasoline tender in the window. India's MRPL offered 35,000mt of 95 RON non-oxy gasoline for Jul 28-29 loading from New Mangalore via a tender that closed Jul 14 same-day — results not yet ascertained (functions as supply-side evidence, not a buy tender).

🌍 Geopolitical Factor

The 92 RON cash differential more than halved w/w (-52.56% to 74¢/b) as China/South Korea cargo supply emerged — explicitly cited by Platts as outweighing the general Hormuz-driven complex strength. Gasoline is the one product in this session where the supply signal beat the geo signal.

Rationale: Gasoline's MOC tape tells a different story than its headline price (+$8.90/b mid, in line with the broader crude/product rally). The one confirmed cargo trade cleared at the lower end of the offer stack, and the pattern of Vitol bid withdrawals across multiple laycans suggests the buy side is not chasing the Hormuz premium the way gasoil and HSFO are. Watch the MRPL tender result and whether China/Korea cargo emergence continues — a second consecutive session of narrowing cash differential would confirm the softening as structural, not one-day noise.

📋 Tender Activity

Iraq's SOMO offering 432,000mt of naphtha for H2 2026 — tender closes Jul 23, 2026. This is the single confirmed product tender across both source documents. Countervailing caution from an Asia-based source: ship scarcity could limit naphtha movement through the Strait of Hormuz "regardless of price."

🌍 Geopolitical Factor

Asian naphtha market was expected to strengthen on Middle East tension per Platts' own daily commentary — consistent with the +$8.54/b Singapore and +$77.00/mt Japan moves — but this has not yet translated into physical cargo trading volume.

Rationale: Price and MOC volume diverge here more than in any other product — naphtha posted one of the largest percentage price gains in the report, yet the physical cargo market (C+F, Middle East, Fujairah) recorded not a single confirmed trade. Treat the bullish price move as sentiment/geo-premium driven rather than demand-confirmed until the SOMO tender clears and physical cargo trading resumes — the ship-scarcity caveat is a genuine near-term supply-chain risk, not just a pricing input.

🌍 Geopolitical Factor

Asia's jet fuel/kerosene complex jumped as the European segment strengthened on Middle East hostilities, explicitly outweighing bearish chatter of discounted Chinese cargoes for late-July loading. South Korea reportedly sold August-loading jet barrels at premiums of 80¢-$1/b to MOPS. M1/M2 backwardation widened to +$6.85/b in midafternoon trading.

Rationale: The +$28.51/b price move is the largest single-day gain of any product in the report, and it is corroborated by swap-spread widening (a genuine Market Structure signal), but the physical MOC book is nearly empty — one offer, no bids, no trades. The bullish call rests on structure and swap data, not confirmed physical clearing; the wide paper bid/offer spread ($146.90 vs $151.30+) is itself informative — market makers are demanding a large premium to sell risk here.
🔵 GASOIL — BULLISH (HIGH CONVICTION)
📈📈 Heaviest confirmed paper volume of the session
📊 MOC Activity

FOB Spore Paper (Aug26 & Bal Month): the most actively traded product paper book in the report. Six+ confirmed trades: Sahara/DVTrading sold to Vitolsg repeatedly at $145.40/bbl (Aug26); Vitolsg sold to Dare 3x at $154.40-$154.60/bbl (Bal Month). Deep two-sided book: bids $143.30-$144.70/bbl, offers $145.50-$147.50/bbl.

Paper Spreads: multiple confirmed Bal Month/Aug26 spread trades at $9.00-$9.20/bbl (Vitolsg, BPSG selling to Dare/Market) — a tightly clustered price cluster indicating high conviction on the spread level itself.

FOB Singapore Cargo (physical, 10/500/2500ppm): zero trades, but an active bid/offer book — Trafigura and Gunvor bidding MOPS +$3.00 to +$4.50 across multiple laycans (up to 160kb-245kb clips); Glencore, Vitol, Unipec offering MOPS +$6.00 to +$7.00. The bid-offer gap ($3-4.50 vs $6-7) is wider than in HSFO, suggesting physical sellers are holding out for a bigger premium than buyers are yet willing to pay.

🌍 Geopolitical Factor

Aug-Sep Singapore gasoil swap time spread surged 50.91% d/d to $8.30/b — near a two-month high (last higher May 20 at $8.50/b) — driven by the Houthis' Bab al-Mandab threat. Cash differential for 10ppm gasoil rose for a third consecutive session, +52¢/b to $2.56/b. Market chatter of a potential South Korean export reduction amid US-Iran escalation adds a second supply-side thread.

Rationale: Two independent signals converge — Market Structure (backwardation surge, near 2-month high) and MOC Volume (the single heaviest-traded paper book of the session) — while the physical cargo bid/offer gap shows sellers pricing in more risk premium than buyers have yet conceded. Highest-conviction call in this brief: structure, volume, and geo narrative all point the same direction with genuine MOC confirmation, not just headline price movement.
