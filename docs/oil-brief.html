🚨 Macro Alert — Houthis Hit Saudi Tankers Layla & Encelia (First Red Sea Attack in a Year); Brent Clears $100/b for First Time Since May 22; Kazakhstan CPC Suspended; Rhine Heading for Record Low

A second major chokepoint opened up today on top of an already-restricted Strait of Hormuz: Yemen's Houthi militants attacked the Saudi-flagged crude tanker Layla and products tanker Encelia in the Red Sea — the first Houthi-linked strike on Red Sea shipping in a year. The Encelia fire was confirmed by Saudi Press Agency (crew safe); Iran has separately told the Houthis to "stand ready to close the Red Sea oil route" should the US strike Iranian infrastructure, and President Trump has vowed to bomb an Iranian bridge or power plant for every future Iranian strike on a ship in Hormuz. Crude cleared a psychological line: ICE Sep Brent settled $100.69/b (+$6.62, +7%) — above $100/b for the first time since May 22 — while NYMEX Sep WTI settled $92.19/b (+$5.36, +6.2%), highest since June 4. Dated Brent hit a seven-week high of $104.92/b and cash Dubai jumped 8.7% to $97.19/b, with the Murban-Dubai premium surging $17.58/b to $24.98/b (highest since April 7). Backwardation blew out on both benchmarks — 6th-month ICE Brent's discount to front-month widened to $17.48/b (widest since May 18), NYMEX WTI's equivalent to $15.47/b (widest since May 20) — a textbook near-term tightness signal layered under the outright spike. Compounding the supply picture: Kazakhstan cut oil output after repeated attacks on tankers loading at the CPC terminal in Novorossiysk forced loadings to be suspended (briefly resumed Jul 20, then re-suspended), with UBS flagging up to 1.8mn b/d of Kazakh crude at risk if the disruption persists. And in Europe, Rhine water levels at the Kaub chokepoint were near 60cm late Jul 23 and forecast to fall toward 43cm within 24 hours, with a sub-20cm — potentially all-time-record — low forecast for Aug 2-6, already forcing LyondellBasell to declare force majeure on its Wesseling butadiene unit and pushing 10ppm diesel FOB ARA barge to $1,319.75/mt, its highest since April 9.
